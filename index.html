Spatial Canvases

Assignment 3 — 2D (p5.js) and 3D (three.js) Spatial Canvases

Design Statement

This work explores the translation of a simple hand-drawn gesture — a single stroke, a filled circle, a scribbled outline — into procedural form. The 2D sketches treat the mark as raw material: a fixed vocabulary of primitives arranged into a composed scene, and a live, responsive trace that keeps changing as it is made. The 3D sketches carry that same idea into space, using primitive geometry, layered materials, light, and fog to give the marks volume, depth, and atmosphere. The final, personal piece pushes this further: it reads the shape of what you actually draw — its roundness, its straightness, its size, where on the page it sits — and uses that to decide what kind of landscape object it becomes, then builds a walkable 3D scene out of it live. Underlying all of this is an interest in where the boundary sits between an authored gesture and a generative system that elaborates on it.

3D Spatial Canvases — three.js

01 — Procedural Geometry, Orbit Camera

A rotating field of primitive geometries (spheres, cones, tori, icosahedra) arranged along a spiral, viewable from any angle with an orbiting camera.

02 — Materials, Lighting & Fog

A small landscape composition exploring standard, physical (glass-like water), and emissive materials under directional + ambient light, with exponential fog for depth.

2D Spatial Canvases — p5.js

03 — Static Composition, Primitive Shapes

A fixed abstract landscape built entirely from circles, arcs, triangles, and lines, using a limited, considered color palette.

04 — Interactive / Animated Drawing

Draw with the mouse — each stroke spawns a trail of drifting, fading particles. Click "clear" to start over.

05 — Personal Exploration: Sketch-to-Landscape

Draw simple shapes and the sketch reads their form to decide what they become: a small round scribble near the top becomes a star, a wider one becomes a sun or a cloud, a short zig-zag becomes a bird. Below the horizon, small round marks become rocks, bigger ones become hills, tall straight strokes become trees, wide ones become mountains, and a wide flat stroke near the bottom becomes water. Pick a shape from the list below and click "Stamp mode" to place more copies of it. Colors are taken exactly as drawn — nothing is re-tinted. Click "Generate Scene" to build a first-person, walkable 3D version below — drag to look around.

Top area: small circle = star, medium circle = sun, flat blob = cloud, short zig-zag = bird. Bottom area: small dot = flower, small blob = rock, medium blob = bush, large blob = hill, tall straight stroke = tree, wide straight stroke = mountain, wide flat stroke near the very bottom = water.
